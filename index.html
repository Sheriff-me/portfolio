Ingredients: 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta) ½ teaspoon salt (optional) ¾ cup warm water (adjust as needed) 1 tablespoon oil or ghee (optional, for softer chapattis) Extra dry flour (for rolling) Step-by-Step Instructions: 1. Make the Dough: a. Mix dry ingredients: In a large bowl or flat mixing dish (parat), add 2 cups of whole wheat flour and salt if using. b. Add water gradually: Slowly pour in warm water while mixing the flour with your hand or spoon. Add a little water at a time to avoid making the dough too wet. c. Knead: Use your hands to knead the mixture into a soft, smooth dough. This usually takes 8–10 minutes. If the dough feels too sticky, add a little flour. If it's too dry, sprinkle more water. d. Rest the dough: Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 20–30 minutes. This helps the gluten relax and makes the chapattis softer. 2. Divide and Roll: a. Make dough balls: Divide the dough into equal-sized balls (golf-ball size is common). b. Dust with dry flour: Lightly coat a ball with dry flour so it doesn’t stick to the rolling pin. c. Roll it out: On a flat surface, roll the ball into a thin, round disc about 6–8 inches wide. Try to make it evenly thin throughout. 3. Cook the Chapatti: a. Preheat the pan: Heat a tava, cast iron skillet, or non-stick pan on medium-high heat until hot. b. Place the chapatti on the pan: Carefully place the rolled dough onto the hot pan. c. First side: Cook for about 30 seconds or until you see bubbles or light spots. d. Flip: Flip the chapatti and cook the second side for 30–40 seconds. Press gently with a spatula to help it cook evenly. e. Second flip (optional puffing): Flip again and cook for another 20–30 seconds, pressing gently. The chapatti may puff up — this is a good sign of softness! f. Remove and keep warm: Remove from pan and place in a covered container or wrap in a clean kitchen towel to keep warm and soft. Optional (for extra softness/flavor): Brush chapattis with ghee or butter after cooking. Serve immediately or store in a covered container to retain softness. Serving Suggestions: Serve chapattis hot with: Lentils (dal) Vegetable curry Meat dishes Yogurt and pickles.
